@section

title: What is Arborito
@/section

Arborito is the Treesys **app** for learning with a map, practice, and games. This demo branch covers the essentials in one sitting.

@section
index: 2
title: Map and folders
@/section

Picture a **folder tree** on screen:

- Each **folder** is a topic (for example "Welcome" or "Games").
- Each **leaf** is a lesson to read and practice.
- The folder **emoji** lives on its card; authors don't need to repeat it in the name.

Tap a leaf to open a lesson. Tap a folder to enter that topic.

@section
index: 3
title: The Forest and your progress
@/section

Everything you import or create shows up in the **Forest** (Forest menu). This "Arborito demo" is a built-in guide: you explore it, but you don't delete or overwrite it; to edit, copy to **My garden**.

By default your **progress** (lessons read, questions passed, streak) stays **on this device**. Exporting a `.arborito` shares the **content**, not your personal history.

@section
index: 4
title: The four Treesys pieces
@/section

Treesys bets on four pieces you'll see in this demo:

1. **Arborito app**: study with a map and outline.
2. **Public courses**: branches other authors share.
3. **Arcade**: mini-games with your own questions.
4. **Python SDK**: optional, for people who generate or validate content outside the app.

You don't need to code to use Arborito. The SDK is for advanced authors and teams.
