# 🌳 The future of Arborito: our roadmap

> "The best time to plant a tree was 20 years ago. The second best time is now."

> **v0.1 alpha** at [arborito.org](https://arborito.org). What follows is a **compass**, not a dated backlog. It changes as the community grows.

Arborito is a **forest of human knowledge** anyone can plant, prune, fork, and walk through, for free. If even one line here resonates, you already belong: teacher, student, translator, or curious visitor.

## Four pillars

Everything we build hangs on four pieces that evolve independently:

| Pillar | Today | Repo |
|--------|-------|------|
| **Arborito** (app) | Lesson maps, editor, memory garden, optional Arcade | [arborito](https://github.com/treesys-org/arborito) |
| **Public courses** | Publish and discover from the app: open network, no central catalogue | (from the app) |
| **Arborito Games** | Browser minigames aware of your lesson context | [arborito-games](https://github.com/treesys-org/arborito-games) |
| **arborito-sdk** | Python SDK for scripts, CLIs, and desktop apps | [arborito-sdk](https://github.com/treesys-org/arborito-sdk) |

## Guiding principles

1. **Knowledge is a right, not a privilege.** Every feature must lower the barrier to education.
2. **Education over institution.** Study what you want, when you want, at the depth you want.
3. **Simplicity is sovereignty.** A student on an old browser in a public library should still be able to contribute.
4. **Community is the ecosystem.** The software is soil; people are the forest.
5. **Decentralisation is resilience.** Knowledge must flow freely, forkable and hostable anywhere. Forks welcome. See [`docs/forking-and-branding.md`](docs/forking-and-branding.md).

## Already growing

Load-bearing parts that work today at [arborito.org](https://arborito.org):

- **Anyone can plant a tree.** Construction Mode: write lessons, branch subtopics, attach quizzes visually, no code, no editorial board.
- **Anyone can publish.** A tree on your device can reach the open network without going through any company. See [`docs/NETWORK.md`](docs/NETWORK.md).
- **Interactive lesson graphs.** Pick a branch, read, quiz, move on.
- **Local-first storage.** Progress on your device by default. No signup, no email, no analytics.
- **Optional online account.** Username + password (no email), sync QR, recovery passphrase under **Privacy & data**.
- **Memory garden.** Spaced repetition when you start to forget.
- **Lesson Arcade** with cartridges (`window.arborito`) and **Python SDK** ([`arborito-sdk`](https://github.com/treesys-org/arborito-sdk)).
- **Web, desktop, mobile.** One codebase; installers on [Releases](https://github.com/treesys-org/arborito/releases).
- **Multilingual UI.** `locales/en/` and `locales/es/`; new languages are a translation away.
- **Optional Sage.** Off by default. Local on desktop; optional Expert mode on web. See [`docs/PRODUCT_GUIDE.md`](docs/PRODUCT_GUIDE.md#web-vs-desktop).

## The journey ahead

### Phase 1: The Seed (_mostly complete_)

Polish, onboarding, and **content** as we open beyond alpha: stability, first-run simplicity, and a broad, diverse lesson base.

### Phase 2: The Sapling (_next_)

- **Collaborative construction.** Real-time multi-author editing, branch roles, peer review on the open network.
- **Sage as augmentation.** Study plans, on-demand quizzes, Socratic mode. Always optional, private-by-default on desktop.
- **Teachers & mentors.** Live classes, office hours, sustainable models for educators inside Arborito.
- **Mission-driven curriculum.** Annual community-voted theme (e.g. climate literacy).

### Phase 3: The Forest (_long-term_)

Federated courses, verifiable credentials, community hubs, paths toward accreditation without a central platform.

## Beyond the horizon

The goal is not to be the "best" educational platform. It is to make the idea of a centralised educational platform obsolete.
